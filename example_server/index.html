About Håkon Wium Lie

Håkon Wium Lie (born July 26, 1965, Halden, Norway) is a web pioneer best known for proposing Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) in 1994 while working at CERN. His original “Cascading HTML Style Sheets” proposal helped separate content from presentation on the web—an idea that reshaped web design. In 1996, the W3C published CSS Level 1 as a recommendation, cementing CSS as a core web standard.

Wium Lie spent the late 1990s developing and promoting CSS at the W3C, co-authoring the book Cascading Style Sheets: Designing for the Web with Bert Bos. He later served as Chief Technology Officer at Opera Software from 1999 to 2016, where he championed web standards (including during the EU's "browser choice" case) and helped push CSS support across browsers.

Today, he is chairman of YesLogic, the company behind Prince, a CSS-based formatter for producing high-quality PDFs from HTML. Academically, he earned an SM (master's) from the MIT Media Lab in 1991 and completed a PhD at the University of Oslo in 2006, focusing on the design and evolution of CSS.
